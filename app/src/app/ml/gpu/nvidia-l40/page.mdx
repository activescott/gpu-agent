# NVIDIA L40 Machine Learning GPU

The NVIDIA L40, a cutting-edge GPU based on the Ada Lovelace architecture, was launched on October 13, 2022. It stands out for its remarkable neural graphics, virtualization, compute, and AI capabilities, tailored for GPU-accelerated data center workloads. With the L40, NVIDIA introduces third-generation RT Cores and an industry-leading 48 GB of GDDR6 memory, elevating real-time ray-tracing performance to new heights. This card is particularly adept at accelerating high-fidelity creative workflows, such as interactive rendering, 3D design, and virtual production. Additionally, the L40 is optimized for 24x7 enterprise data center operations and boasts enterprise-grade components and power-efficient hardware. The GPU's design includes secure boot with internal root of trust, ensuring top-tier performance and reliability for a variety of data center workloads.

## Specifications

- **GPU Architecture**: NVIDIA Ada Lovelace Architecture
- **Memory Size**: 48 GB GDDR6 with ECC
- **Memory Bandwidth**: 864GB/s
- **Tensor Cores**: 568 (4th Generation)
- **GPU Clock Rate**: Base Clock 735 MHz, Boost Clock up to 2490 MHz
- **Max Power Consumption**: 300W
- **FP32 TFLOPS**: 90.5 TFLOPS
- **Compute Performance**: Data science and AI model training has been improved with 568 4th Generation Tensor Cores providing up to 90.1 TF32 TFLOPS

The L40's performance is further enhanced by its AI-enabled graphics, video, and image generation capabilities, making it a powerful tool for creating high-quality images and immersive visual content. The GPU's proficiency in streaming and video content workloads, including AV1 encoding, adds to its impressive feature set. Furthermore, the L40's training and inference performance, combined with its stability and reliability, make it an ideal platform for single-GPU AI training and development.
